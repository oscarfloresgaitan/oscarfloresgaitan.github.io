About

My name is Oscar and I am a final-semester undergraduate Physics student at Universidad del Valle de Guatemala. I have a strong interest in astronomy and astrophysics, particularly in developing and applying methods to discover and characterize exoplanets and search for a potential "second Earth". I am currently a research intern at the Department of Physics & Astronomy at the University of Delaware working with Prof. Sally Dodson-Robinson.

I plan to pursue a graduate degree in astronomy or astrophysics, with the goal of contributing to the discovery and characterization of new planetary systems and the search for potentially Earth-like worlds.

Outside of academics, I am a sports enthusiast. I train for triathlon, play soccer as a goalkeeper, and enjoy doing calisthenics. I recently completed an Ironman 70.3, and previously played professional soccer, where I became a two-time national scholar champion.

Research

My research interests include exoplanet detection and characterization, planet formation and evolution, and the application of machine learning and statistical methods to astronomical time-series and spectral data.

I am currently working as a research intern at the University of Delaware with Prof. Sally Dodson-Robinson on analyzing and developing mathematical methods to distinguish genuine exoplanet discoveries from stellar noise. This research focuses on improving the reliability of planet detections in radial velocity (RV) data affected by stellar variability, helping to ensure that candidate signals correspond to genuine planets rather than artifacts of stellar activity or instrumental effects.

I am currently working on two projects:

Barnard's Star Planetary System

This project is a follow-up analysis of the confirmed four-planet system around Barnard's Star, using frequency-domain techniques, Gaussian Process (GP) modeling, exploration of different orbital configurations, and dynamical stability analysis to understand the system's architecture better.

ESSP IV – Extreme Stellar Signals Project IV

I am participating in ESSP IV, an international collaboration and friendly competition among teams developing methods to distinguish true exoplanet signals from stellar variability and instrumental noise.

Previous Research:


Detection and Characterization of Exoplanets with TESS

I worked with Prof. Eduardo Rubio-Herrera in the generation and analysis of transit data to detect and characterize exoplanets. This includes developing an algorithm using TESS data that applies Gaussian Process (GP) modeling to identify planetary signals.
